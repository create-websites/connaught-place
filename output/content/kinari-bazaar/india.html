Market overview

A narrow alley plays a significant role in Delhi's bustling wedding industry.

Nestled in the labyrinth of Shahjahanabad's Chandni Chowk, Kinari Bazaar is located near Paranthe Wali Gali, Dariba Kalan, Nai Sarak, and Gurudwara Sis Ganj Sahib. The market's namesake, "kinari," signifies its specialty in luxurious trimmings like gold and silver borders, embroidered ribbons, brocade, mirrors, sequins, beads, tassels, and other embellishments favored by boutique owners, tailors, event planners, artisans, and families getting ready for special occasions.

What to buy in Kinari Bazaar

To fully explore the market, it is recommended to shop by category. Although shops may appear small from the outside, they often have extensive inventories filling every corner, so be sure to bring reference photos, fabric swatches, and measurements.

Textile trims

  • Zari, brocade, gota patti and metallic borders
  • Crochet lace, net lace, velvet trims and piping
  • Mirror-work, bead-work and sequin strips
  • Decorative patches for lehengas, sarees and dupattas

Wedding accessories

  • Latkans, tassels, kalire-style hangings and dori ends
  • Shagun envelopes, potli bags and ring platters
  • Decorated trays, thalis, boxes and return-gift packaging
  • Invitation embellishments and craft supplies

Festival and pooja decor

  • Idol poshak trims, tiny garlands and decorative fabrics
  • Diwali torans, rakhi material and seasonal craft pieces
  • Artificial flowers, ribbons and ornate packaging

Fashion add-ons

  • Imitation jewellery, hair accessories and decorative clips
  • Beads, buttons and stones for DIY embellishment
  • Bulk craft material for boutiques and online sellers

MetroChandni Chowk station is the primary entry point on the Yellow Line, while Lal Qila and Chawri Bazar stations are also viable options depending on your walking path.
TimingsTypically, stores are open from late morning until evening, usually between 10/11 AM to 8 PM, although specific hours may vary by shop.
ClosedMany lanes and shops in Chandni Chowk and Kinari Bazaar typically close on Sundays, but some may have different operating hours.
Best timeWeekday mornings are the best time for serious shopping, while October to March is the most comfortable season for walking.
Entry feeThere is no fee for entry; you only pay for shopping, food, rickshaw rides, or guided walks.

Smart shopping tips

Carry swatches and measurements

When shopping for borders and lace, make sure to know the required length and bring the fabric color with you as shop lights may alter shades.

Bargain, but compare first

Prices can differ based on quality, width, quantity, and finish. Be sure to compare prices at a couple of stores before making a bulk purchase.

Use cash plus UPI

While many stores take digital payments, cash may be more convenient for smaller vendors and cheaper items.

Travel light

The lanes are congested and tight. Opt for a sling bag, comfy shoes, and steer clear of bulky luggage.
